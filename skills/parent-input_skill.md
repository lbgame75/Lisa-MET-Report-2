PARENT INPUT — General Education / Special Education Students
PURPOSE
Write a clean, natural Parent Input section for any special education evaluation, including initials and reevaluations, using parent-provided information only. The final section should sound like a real school psychologist wrote it, not like a transcript and not like an AI summary.
NONNEGOTIABLE RULES

Use strict source-voice
Preserve the parent's meaning, priorities, and tone
Do not add interpretation, analysis, themes, comparisons to school data, or evaluator opinion
Do not invent skills, concerns, developmental history, or descriptive language
Do not soften concerns
Do not add clinical phrasing unless the parent used it or it is necessary to accurately name something the parent directly reported, such as a diagnosis, therapy, or medication
Do not use em dashes
Do not use the word "reflects"
Do not make the writing sound mechanical, overpolished, or generic

YOU MAY

Reorder information for flow
Combine related parent statements into clear narrative sentences
Correct minor grammar for readability
Use pronouns and transitions naturally
Condense repetitive parent wording while preserving meaning
Use attribution strategically so it is always clear the information came from the parent without sounding repetitive
Preserve direct quotes when the parent used specific language that would be softened or lost by paraphrasing. If the parent said "free time," write "free time." If the parent said "taming his emotions," do not substitute "managing" or "regulating." Stay close to their actual words, especially when those words are plain and specific.

YOU MAY NOT

Turn the section into interpretation
Summarize "themes"
Generalize beyond the parent's statements
Add school-based meaning unless the parent directly stated it
Repeat "According to his/her mother" or "Parent reported" at the start of every paragraph

STRUCTURE
Opening line — use exactly:
Parent input was obtained on [DATE] through a parent questionnaire/interview completed by [NAME], [RELATIONSHIP].
Paragraph 1:

Strengths
Interests
Personality traits or positive qualities if directly stated
General functioning at home
Daily routines
Areas of growth or improvement

Paragraph 2:

Communication
Social interaction
Behavior and emotional regulation
Attention
Learning style
Supports or strategies that help

Paragraph 3:

Adaptive or self-help skills
Independence
Safety concerns
Medical or developmental updates
Diagnoses, medications, or therapies if reported

Paragraph 4 (if needed):

Family priorities and goals
Parent concerns about school
Outside services and supports
Additional information the parent wants the team to know

INITIAL EVALUATIONS
If the parent provides developmental history, early concerns, medical history, preschool history, or family context, integrate that naturally into the most appropriate paragraph rather than creating a separate labeled subsection unless explicitly requested.
REEVALUATIONS
Emphasize current functioning, changes since the last evaluation, ongoing concerns, and current priorities while keeping the same narrative style and flow. When the parent indicates growth or progress since the last evaluation — even implicitly — name that as a reevaluation frame early in the section. End with a distinct closing paragraph that captures the parent's stated priorities for the coming year, using their language as closely as possible. Do not fold priorities into earlier paragraphs.
STYLE REQUIREMENTS

Write in Lisa's natural, professional, parent-friendly voice
Use smooth narrative with short to medium declarative sentences
Keep authorship clear so the reader always knows this is parent-reported information
Do not start multiple paragraphs the same way
Vary attribution naturally, for example:

"Gabe's mother reported…"
"She shared…"
"His mother noted…"
"At home, …"
"Social situations that are harder for him include…"


Once source is established, do not over-attribute every sentence

QUALITY TARGET
The section should read like a polished MET narrative based only on parent report. It should have the same clean flow and readability across both initial evaluations and reevaluations. It should feel organized, human, and specific without drifting into interpretation.
OUTPUT
Produce only the Parent Input section narrative, ready to paste directly into the MET report.
