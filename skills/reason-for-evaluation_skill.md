## INSTRUCTIONS
### Reason for Evaluation

---

### YOUR JOB
This is a source document section. Take language directly from the REED. Do not interpret, editorialize, or add clinical framing. The job is to tell the reader who the student is, what the team already decided, and what this evaluation was for.

---

### STRUCTURE

All four components run together in a single paragraph. No line breaks between them. No numbered sections. No separate closing sentence.

**1. Opening sentence**
Student first name, grade, school, and current eligibility category. Use first name only — do not repeat the full name. Do not add a second sentence orienting the reader to the report (e.g., "This report documents the findings of..."). Go directly from student identification to the REED.

**2. REED determination**
What data the team determined was and was not needed, and why. Use REED language directly. Do not paraphrase or reframe. If the REED states that no additional data is needed to confirm continued eligibility, say exactly that.

**3. Testing requested + purpose (combined)**
State what testing was requested and weave the evaluation purpose directly into that sentence. Do not state purpose as a separate sentence. 

Example: *"Reading and math testing was requested to update her present levels of academic performance and to inform current IEP goal development."*

Standard purposes include:
- Update present levels of performance (PLAFP)
- Inform current IEP goal development
- Determine whether modifications to services are warranted

State whichever purposes apply. Use the language Lisa or the REED provides. Do not add purposes that were not stated.

**4. Current placement and services (if requested)**
If Lisa includes placement and services, insert after the opening sentence and before the REED determination. If Lisa says to omit it, leave it out entirely — do not substitute or approximate.

---

### CRITICAL RULES

**DO THIS:**
- Use first name only throughout the section
- Run all components together in one paragraph — no breaks
- Take language directly from the REED where provided
- Preserve REED language — do not smooth or reframe it
- Weave purpose into the testing sentence, not as a standalone
- Keep the section short — this is orientation, not analysis
- End when the testing purpose is stated — no wrap-up sentence

**DO NOT DO THIS:**
- Do not open with or insert "This report documents..." or any equivalent orientation sentence
- Do not use the student's full name after the opening sentence
- Do not use referral language or suspected disability framing — eligibility is already established
- Do not add ruling-out language or eligibility category considerations
- Do not soften, expand, or reframe the REED determination
- Do not add clinical interpretation of any kind
- Do not add a closing sentence that links to planning or evaluation findings
- Do not use em dashes

---

### GOLD STANDARD EXAMPLE

> Avery is a seventh-grade student at West Middle School currently receiving special education services under the Specific Learning Disability (SLD) eligibility category. A Review of Existing Evaluation Data (REED) was completed on March 10, 2026. The team determined that sufficient data existed to continue Avery's eligibility under the SLD category and that no additional assessment was needed to confirm continued eligibility. Reading and math testing was requested to update her present levels of academic performance and to inform current IEP goal development.

---

### VOICE NOTE
This section is plain and factual. It does not require clinical voice — it requires accuracy. Short declarative sentences. No wrap-up. No interpretation. The section ends when the testing purpose is stated.
