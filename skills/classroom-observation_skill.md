INSTRUCTIONS
Summarize Direct Classroom Observation of Student Functioning
WHEN TO USE THIS SKILL
You've conducted a direct observation of the student in their classroom and need to summarize what you observed.
WHAT THE USER WILL PROVIDE

Student name, grade, and classroom/class
Date and duration of observation
Class/instruction context
Specific observations:

Attention and engagement
Interaction with peers
Interaction with teacher
Response to academic tasks
Physical behaviors
Emotional regulation
Work completion and accuracy
Following directions
Any behavioral incidents


Behavioral notes: Specific examples

YOUR JOB
Create a narrative observation section that:

Sets the context
Describes attention and engagement
Describes interaction with peers
Describes interaction with teacher
Describes response to academic tasks
Describes emotional regulation
Describes behavior and physical functioning
Makes connections to evaluation questions

STRUCTURE
Opening paragraph: Set the context

"Classroom observation was conducted on [date] in [student]'s [grade/subject] class with [teacher name]."
"The observation occurred during [type of instruction] and lasted [duration]."

Attention and Engagement paragraph:

How much of the time was he focused?
What happened when he was off-task?
What captured his attention?
Specific examples with timing

Peer Interaction paragraph (if observed):

Did he initiate interaction?
How did peers respond to him?
Was interaction appropriate or problematic?
Was he socially included or isolated?

Teacher Interaction paragraph:

How did he respond to teacher instruction?
Did he ask for help appropriately?
How did he respond to feedback or redirection?
Was he compliant with expectations?

Response to Academic Tasks paragraph:

What kind of tasks?
Did he engage with the tasks?
Work quality and accuracy?
How did he respond to difficulty?

Emotional Regulation and Behavior paragraph:

Was he calm or dysregulated?
How did he respond to frustration or difficulty?
Were there any behavioral incidents?
Did he show age-appropriate classroom behavior?

Physical Functioning paragraph (if relevant):

Was he restless or still?
Motor control issues?
Handwriting?
Physical limitations that affected task performance?

Summary and Connection to Referral paragraph:

How do these observations relate to the referral concern?

LISA'S VOICE RULES
DO THIS

Use specific time references
Use specific examples of behavior
Describe what he actually did
Compare to peer norms
State objective observations before interpretation
Use neutral language

DO NOT DO THIS

No em dashes
Do not editorialize or interpret without first stating what you observed
Do not compare him to other students by name
Do not make assumptions about motivation or intent
Do not soften serious behavioral incidents
Do not generalize
