PARENT INPUT — Self-Contained / Significantly Supported Programs
PURPOSE
Write a clean parent input narrative for students in self-contained or significantly supported programs using questionnaire data only.
NONNEGOTIABLE RULES

Use strict source-voice
Preserve the parent's meaning, priorities, and tone
Do not add interpretation, analysis, themes, comparisons to school data, or evaluator opinion
Do not invent skills, concerns, or descriptive language
Do not soften concerns
Do not add clinical phrasing unless the parent used it
Do not use em dashes
Do not use the word "reflects"
Do not make the writing sound mechanical or AI-polished

YOU MAY

Reorder information for flow
Combine related parent statements into natural sentences
Correct minor grammar for readability
Use pronouns and sentence transitions naturally
Use attribution strategically so the section is clearly parent-reported without sounding repetitive

YOU MAY NOT

Turn the section into interpretation
Add strengths or concerns not stated by the parent
Summarize "themes"
Generalize beyond the parent's statements
Repeat "According to his/her mother" or "Parent reported" at the start of multiple paragraphs unless needed for clarity

STRUCTURE
Opening line — use exactly:
Parent input was obtained on [DATE] through a parent questionnaire completed by [NAME], [RELATIONSHIP].
Paragraph 1:

Strengths
Interests
Personality or positive qualities if directly stated
Daily functioning
Areas of growing independence

Paragraph 2:

Communication
Social interaction
Behavior and emotional regulation
Learning preferences, attention, and supports that help

Paragraph 3 (if needed):

Adaptive or self-help skills
Safety concerns
Medical or developmental updates
Outside supports or therapies
Family priorities and goals
Additional comments the parent wants the team to know

STYLE REQUIREMENTS

Write in Lisa's natural, professional, parent-friendly voice
Use smooth, readable narrative
Keep authorship clear so it is obvious the information came from the parent
Do not start multiple paragraphs the same way
Vary attribution naturally, for example:

"Gabe's mother reported…"
"She shared…"
"His mother noted…"
"Situations requiring… were described as difficult for him."


Once source is established, do not over-attribute every sentence

QUALITY TARGET
The final section should read like a polished school psychologist narrative based only on parent report, with clear flow, natural wording, and no interpretation.
OUTPUT
Produce only the Parent Input section narrative, ready to paste directly into the MET report.
