Write the Review of Previous Evaluations section for a MET report. This is a background snapshot for the report reader showing what evaluations have been done, what was found, and what support has been in place.
BIOGRAPHICAL/MEDICAL OPENER:
When significant biographical or medical context precedes the evaluation history (adoption, congenital conditions, limited early records, immigration, or other circumstances that frame everything that follows), state this first in a brief standalone paragraph before the first evaluation. This is not required for every student. Use it when the background is needed to make sense of the evaluation history.
OPENING LINE CONVENTION — the first evaluation paragraph should begin with one of these:

"[Student] was initially evaluated for special education services at age [X] / in [grade]..." (when school-based evaluation records are available)
"[Student] was first evaluated in the clinical setting..." (when only outside evaluation records are present)

FORMAT: Chronological narrative paragraphs only. No bullets or lists.
DENSITY RULE — THIS IS CRITICAL:
Each evaluation paragraph should read like a clinical summary of what was found and why it matters, with just enough score specificity to anchor the findings. Do not inventory every score from every measure. Name the instruments, state the most important scores (FSIQ, key composites, adaptive composites), and describe the pattern of findings in narrative form. If a cluster of related findings tells a story, describe the cluster, not each individual data point. The goal is: a team member reads this and understands what each evaluation found and what it meant for the student. The goal is not: a team member reads this and has a complete score reference for every measure ever administered.
For each evaluation, include:

When it was done and by whom (agency/district, not individual evaluator names)
Why the student was evaluated
What the general findings showed, anchored in key scores
What eligibility was determined or what diagnosis was given
What placement or services resulted

CURRENT IEP:
A brief statement of placement, program, and goal areas by domain is sufficient. Detailed goal language, accommodations lists, and progress report findings are not needed in this section.
Do NOT include:

Full score tables or score inventories
Evaluator names
Closure sentence at the end of the section
Data from the current evaluation
Eligibility analysis or recommendations
Interpretive commentary connecting findings across evaluations (that belongs in Summary)

ACCURACY RULE: Every score, date, and factual claim must be verified against the source document before delivering the section. If a score or date cannot be confirmed from the provided records, do not include it. Do not attribute data to an evaluation unless you can verify which evaluation produced it.
Stop when the history is told.
