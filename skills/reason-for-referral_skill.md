YOUR JOB
This is a source document section — preserve the original meaning and tone, no interpretation.
You will:

Extract the relevant information from the referral
Organize it clearly for a report audience
Preserve the language and framing from the original referral
State what the referral says, not what you think about it

Format: "[Student] is a [grade] student [referred/due for reevaluation] to [reason] so the team can determine [what]."
Then: State the suspected areas of disability or eligibility categories being considered.
STRUCTURE
Opening sentence: Name, grade, type of evaluation
Reason for referral: Why is this student being evaluated?

"Student is referred at parent request"
"Student is referred due to concerns about..."
"Student is due for reevaluation to..."

Current services/placement: If applicable
Specific concerns or suspected areas: What areas of disability are being considered?
Note about areas NOT being considered: If the referral explicitly excludes a category
CRITICAL RULES
DO THIS

Take language directly from the referral document.
Preserve that language
Keep the original meaning and emphasis

DO NOT DO THIS

Do NOT flag or rewrite the referral language
Do NOT add your own interpretation
Do NOT editorialize or add clinical framing
Do NOT change the tone of the original referral
Do NOT soften or amplify concerns
