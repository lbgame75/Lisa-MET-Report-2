Communication  
Both raters reported a Communication standard score of 20 (\<1st percentile). On the Parent form, Receptive and Expressive subdomains yielded age equivalents of approximately 1 year, 3 months, with Written skills below 3 years. On the Teacher form, Receptive and Expressive skills were below 3 years, and Written skills were approximately 4 years.

Item-level ratings indicate that Suma demonstrates a small number of early foundational communication skills. Across raters, early items endorsed include responding to his name, understanding a limited number of familiar words, following very simple routine-based directions, and using single words to label or request. However, items reflecting more advanced language development were consistently rated as not demonstrated. These include answering basic “who,” “what,” or “why” questions, following multi-step directions, describing events, using phrases or sentences consistently, reading simple text, or writing meaningful words. Written communication items were largely rated as not yet present. Overall, the item pattern reflects communication functioning within the early toddler developmental range.

Daily Living Skills  
Daily Living Skills also fall in the Extremely Low range across both settings (Parent SS \= 27; Teacher SS \= 20). On the Parent form, Personal skills were rated at approximately 8 months, with Community skills below 3 years. Domestic skills were relatively stronger within the profile but remain below age expectations. On the Teacher form, Personal, Numeric, and School Community subdomains were all below 3 years.

Item-level responses indicate that Suma independently performs a limited number of early self-care tasks, such as feeding himself with utensils and participating in basic hygiene routines. However, items reflecting age-expected independence were largely rated as not demonstrated. These include managing materials independently, understanding time concepts, handling money, completing tasks without adult direction, and functioning independently in community or school-based routines. The overall pattern reflects isolated early self-care skills with significant dependence in broader adaptive functioning.

Socialization  
Both Parent and Teacher ratings yielded a Socialization standard score of 20 (\<1st percentile). On the Parent form, Interpersonal Relationships and Play and Leisure were rated at approximately 0 years, with Coping Skills below 2 years. On the Teacher form, Interpersonal Relationships, Play and Leisure, and Coping Skills were all below 3 years.

Item responses indicate that only a small number of early social behaviors were endorsed, such as brief shared attention or participation in simple interactive routines when prompted. Items reflecting age-appropriate social development were consistently rated as not demonstrated. These include initiating peer interaction, engaging in cooperative or imaginative play, understanding social rules, negotiating conflict, and demonstrating independent coping strategies. The pattern reflects social functioning within the early developmental range with limited reciprocal interaction.
