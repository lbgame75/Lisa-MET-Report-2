**PARENT INPUT**  
Eva’s mother described her as a strong reader who is kind, respectful, and a good friend to others. She is organized, a talented artist, enjoys gaming, and has a deep love for animals and science. Her mother views her as a “big thinker” with creativity and empathy, especially when helping friends or caring for animals. At home, Eva is characterized as a sweet, sensitive young woman who thrives on structure and predictability.

Despite these strengths, her mother expressed significant concern that Eva is currently struggling across domains. She stated that Eva is behind socially, emotionally, and academically, and that she often lacks internal drive to sustain her effort. Consequences and rewards have not consistently motivated her, and her mother explained, “She isn’t at risk of falling through the cracks, she is already at the bottom trying to claw her way back up.” Schoolwork and homework are described as overwhelming, often leading to shutdowns. Eva tends to start tasks but loses stamina, shifting to something else before the original task is complete. This frequently results in unfinished work piling up, which compounds her stress and disengagement the following day.

Her mother reports that Eva requires frequent breaks and benefits from supportive check-ins to identify priorities and break down assignments into manageable steps. However, even with this scaffolding, she often tires quickly and shuts down if the workload feels too large. The transition back to in-person learning this year, after being out since her freshman year, has been particularly challenging.

Socially, Eva does best at home with her family and maintains a couple of long-standing friendships from elementary school. These friendships continue, though contact is limited. Her mother noted that peer interactions at school are very difficult, and that “there are no social situations that are easy for Eva.” She emphasized that simply being able to enter school each day without a panic attack or meltdown is an achievement that reflects Eva’s hard work and progress in managing anxiety.

From a health and developmental standpoint, Eva’s pregnancy and delivery were without complication, and her developmental milestones were generally within normal limits, though she did begin speaking later than expected. She has never been hospitalized and has not experienced major illness or injury. Her medical diagnoses include Autism Spectrum Disorder (Level 1), Anxiety Disorder, and ARFID. She takes Prozac, which her mother reports has had a significantly positive impact on her anxiety and overall functioning compared to when she was unmedicated. She also has severe myopia, for which she is monitored by a specialist. Eating consistently during the school day remains a major priority, as Eva does not always independently complete meals or snacks.

Looking ahead, her mother identified several priorities: increasing daily school attendance, reducing anxiety through repeated exposure and reframing strategies, supporting Eva in handling difficult academic and social situations more effectively, and ensuring she meets her nutritional needs during the school day. She emphasized that patience, understanding, structure, and predictability are key for Eva’s success. Her mother expressed cautious optimism about the future, noting, “We know we have a long way to go, but every step is a step in the right direction.”
