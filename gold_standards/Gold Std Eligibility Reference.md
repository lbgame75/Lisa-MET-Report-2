**Eligibility Reference Guide**

This reference provides standard criteria and narrative frameworks for determining special education eligibility under various categories. 

### **Specific Learning Disability (SLD)**
To be eligible under the category of Specific Learning Disability, the following criteria must be met:
1. **Inadequate Achievement:** The student does not achieve adequately for their age or to meet State-approved grade-level standards in one or more of the following areas: Oral Expression, Listening Comprehension, Written Expression, Basic Reading Skill, Reading Fluency Skills, Reading Comprehension, Mathematics Calculation, or Mathematics Problem Solving.
2. **Pattern of Strengths and Weaknesses (PSW) or Lack of Response to Intervention (RTI):** 
   - The student exhibits a pattern of strengths and weaknesses in performance, achievement, or both, relative to age, State-approved grade-level standards, or intellectual development.
   - OR, the student fails to make sufficient progress to meet age or State-approved grade-level standards when using a process based on the student’s response to scientific, research-based intervention.
3. **Exclusionary Factors:** The findings are not primarily the result of: a visual, hearing, or motor disability; intellectual disability; emotional disturbance; cultural factors; environmental or economic disadvantage; or limited English proficiency.
4. **Observation:** The student was observed in the learning environment to document academic performance and behavior in the area of difficulty.

### **Other Health Impairment (OHI)**
Eligibility for Other Health Impairment requires:
1. **Medical Condition:** Having limited strength, vitality, or alertness, including a heightened alertness to environmental stimuli, that results in limited alertness with respect to the educational environment.
2. **Chronic or Acute:** The condition is due to chronic or acute health problems such as asthma, attention deficit disorder or attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, diabetes, epilepsy, a heart condition, hemophilia, lead poisoning, leukemia, nephritis, rheumatic fever, sickle cell anemia, or Tourette syndrome.
3. **Adverse Educational Impact:** The condition adversely affects the student's educational performance.

### **Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)**
Eligibility for Autism requires:
1. **Social Communication/Interaction:** Persistent deficits in social communication and social interaction across multiple contexts (e.g., social-emotional reciprocity, nonverbal communicative behaviors, developing/maintaining relationships).
2. **Restricted/Repetitive Patterns:** Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior, interests, or activities (e.g., stereotyped motor movements, insistence on sameness, highly restricted fixated interests, hyper- or hypo-reactivity to sensory input).
3. **Developmental Period:** Symptoms must be present in the early developmental period.
4. **Adverse Educational Impact:** The disability adversely affects the student's educational performance.

### **Emotional Disability (ED)**
Eligibility for Emotional Disability requires one or more of the following characteristics over a long period of time and to a marked degree:
1. An inability to learn that cannot be explained by intellectual, sensory, or health factors.
2. An inability to build or maintain satisfactory interpersonal relationships with peers and teachers.
3. Inappropriate types of behavior or feelings under normal circumstances.
4. A general pervasive mood of unhappiness or depression.
5. A tendency to develop physical symptoms or fears associated with personal or school problems.
6. **Adverse Educational Impact:** The condition adversely affects the student's educational performance.

### **Intellectual Disability (ID)**
Eligibility for Intellectual Disability requires:
1. **Cognitive Functioning:** Significantly subaverage general intellectual functioning (typically an IQ score of 70 or below).
2. **Adaptive Behavior:** Concurrent deficits in adaptive behavior (conceptual, social, and practical skills).
3. **Developmental Period:** Manifested during the developmental period.
4. **Adverse Educational Impact:** The disability adversely affects the student's educational performance.

**Standard Narrative Template for Eligibility Statement:**
"Based on the results of this comprehensive evaluation, the multidisciplinary team has determined that [Student Name] meets the criteria for eligibility as a student with a [Disability Category]. Specifically, [Student Name] demonstrates [summarize key findings, e.g., a pattern of strengths and weaknesses in reading comprehension and fluid reasoning]. These deficits are not primarily the result of exclusionary factors and have been documented to adversely impact [Student Name]'s educational performance in the areas of [list areas, e.g., literacy and independent task completion]. Specialized instruction and supports are required to address these needs."
