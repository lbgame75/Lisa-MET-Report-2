*Reading:*  
Blair’s reading performance showed a split between basic reading skills and comprehension. His word reading fell in the Average range,  his oral reading fluency was Low Average, with smooth pacing but limited phrasing, stress, and natural intonation, and his comprehension was Below Average. He was accurate on only about half of literal, inferential, and expository questions, a pattern that indicates difficulty retaining information, identifying central ideas, and integrating details. He often needed to reread passages to answer questions and had trouble explaining what the text was mostly about. His performance suggests that he may learn more effectively when reading tasks are broken into smaller steps, when key ideas are previewed or revisited, and when he receives guidance in identifying main ideas and monitoring for understanding as he reads. Taken together, these scores show that while Blair can read individual words well, he struggles to construct meaning from extended text without support.

*Math*:  
Blair’s math skills were his lowest area of performance. Both numerical operations and math problem solving fell in the Below Average to Well Below Average range. He demonstrated automaticity with some single-digit addition, subtraction, and multiplication facts, but struggled with nearly all multi-step or procedural tasks. He was unable to borrow consistently in multi-digit subtraction, could not complete multi-digit multiplication, and did not demonstrate functional division skills. He was also unable to order fractions, convert fractions to decimals, solve fraction word problems, solve simple equations or apply geometric concepts such as perimeter or angle relationships. On problem-solving tasks, foundational gaps prevented him from understanding what the problem required or identifying the steps needed to reach a solution. His performance suggests that he may need instruction that revisits foundational number concepts, reduces memory and processing demands, and teaches multi-step procedures through graduated practice with immediate feedback. Blair’s math results align with his classroom functioning and reflect significant difficulty accessing grade-level math content without intensive support.

*Written Language:*  
Blair’s written expression skills were Below Average across sentence-level and essay tasks. On sentence composition, he benefited from having the directions remind him about capitalization and punctuation, but these conventions faded as the task continued. His writing pace was faster than his language organization, leading to omitted words, incomplete thoughts, and missed punctuation. His essay composition was about his favorite game,and he chose Fortnite. Blair wrote enthusiastically and with a conversational tone, but his ideas were loosely organized and lacked clear sentence boundaries, with inconsistent capitalization, run-ons, and limited elaboration. He expressed his thoughts freely but had difficulty structuring them in a way that conveyed a clear main idea with supporting detail. This pattern suggests that he may make better progress when writing instruction emphasizes planning routines, explicit modeling of sentence and paragraph structure, and guided practice that slows down the pace of composition so he can coordinate mechanics, organization, and clarity. His writing reflects strengths in voice and engagement but weaknesses in the underlying skills needed to produce organized and accurate written work.
