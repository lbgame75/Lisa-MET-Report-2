Educational History  
Blair’s educational record from fifth grade through eighth grade shows a student with consistently strong attendance (97%+) who has faced ongoing challenges with organization, attention, and independent academic performance. In fifth grade, teachers noted difficulty following directions, managing materials, sustaining focus, and completing work without direct support. He struggled to stay engaged during instruction, and his foundational reading, writing, and math skills developed more slowly than expected. Teacher comments highlighted the need for step-by-step support, repeated prompting, and assistance with summarizing text, forming complete sentences, and managing multi step math tasks.

As Blair entered middle school, these patterns continued. His sixth and seventh grade report cards reflected mostly C and D level performance in core classes. Teachers across subjects described inconsistent engagement, low test and quiz performance, and difficulty starting and sustaining work without adult intervention. Despite good attendance, he often arrived unprepared or without necessary materials and needed repeated reminders to follow expectations. Some improvement was noted in hands-on or structured environments, where he could participate more readily, but independent academic tasks remained challenging.

Current eighth grade performance shows a similar profile. Blair is passing most classes but continues to struggle with missing assignments, lower assessment performance, and difficulty following instructions. Teachers describe him as cooperative when redirected yet frequently unprepared and needing additional support to understand and complete classroom tasks. His MSTEP scores in both ELA and Math have been “Not Proficient” for the past three years, and NWEA data reflect slow and uneven growth, with reading in the Average to Low Average range and math in the Below Average range. His Lexile level indicates that accessing grade level text is challenging without support.
