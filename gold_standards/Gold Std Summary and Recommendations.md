**SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS**  
Violet is a tenth grade student who was referred for an initial special education evaluation at parent request to better understand her learning profile, classroom performance, and emotional functioning in order to determine whether she requires specially designed instruction.

In the classroom setting, teachers consistently describe Violet as polite, socially connected, and generally positive in demeanor. She participates more readily in small groups and when tasks feel manageable. At the same time, themes across teachers include frequent absences and tardies, missing assignments, limited initiation of work, minimal help-seeking, and declining academic performance when tasks require sustained effort or independent reasoning. Even with extended time, scaffolded instruction, flexible deadlines, and other accommodations provided through her Section 504 Plan, her work completion and depth of understanding remain inconsistent. Attendance and task initiation are significant factors affecting her academic performance.

Cognitive testing indicates overall ability in the Below Average range (WISC-V Full Scale IQ \= 81; 10th percentile), with weakness in Verbal Comprehension (7th percentile) Fluid Reasoning (4th percentile) and Long-Term Retrieval (3rd percentile). Working memory and processing speed are relative strengths. Achievement testing revealed adequate foundational skills in word reading, basic computation, and structured writing. However, Violet struggled significantly with comprehension, applied reasoning, word problems, and tasks requiring independent organization of ideas. Her underachievement is consistent with her cognitive profile and with a documented history of chronic absenteeism, which has limited consistent exposure to instruction over multiple years. Because her academic performance aligns with both her overall ability level and significant instructional gaps related to attendance, her difficulties are not considered unexpected. For this reason, eligibility under Specific Learning Disability is not supported.

Based on observation, teacher feedback, and BASC rating scale Violet does not meet eligibility criteria for Emotional Impairment. Violet reports having friends demonstrating the ability to build and maintain relationships. Violet has only one documented incidence of inappropriate behavior in the school setting from 5 years ago. Violet does express some anxiety related to school but not to the level one would consider pervasive. Violet has had some attendance issues with not wanting to come to school that may be related fears but she has made progress in that area this school year. At this time, the suspected disability of Emotional Impairment does not interfere with access to and progress in general education to the degree that the student **requires** special education programs/services. Just because Violet does not meet the criteria at this time it does not diminish that she struggles with anxiety and depression. Regular check-ins with her assigned counselor and continued supports offered through her 504 or an IEP are recommended. 

Based on observation, teacher feedback and Conners rating scale Violet does not meet eligibility for Otherwise Health Impairment for ADHD. Violet does not demonstrate a limited strength, vitality, or alertness, including a heightened alertness to environmental stimuli, which results in limited alertness with respect to the educational environment. At this time, the suspected disability of Otherwise Health Impairment does not interfere with Violett’s access to and progress in general education to the degree that the student **requires** special education programs/services. Again this does not mean that teachers don’t see the impact of ADHD on Violet, it is just not to the severity of meeting the criteria. Regular check-ins with her assigned counselor and continued supports offered through her 504 or an IEP are recommended. 

Speech and language evaluation results indicate that Violet struggled with all areas of expressive and receptive language. She especially had difficulty with phonemic awareness,  listening comprehension,  reading comprehension, and vocabulary awareness.  Grammar also seems to be an area of concern as shown by the oral and written language samples. Based on the assessments and language samples, Violet does meet eligibility for a Speech and Language Impairment to the extend that it requires special education programs/services.. 

A final determination regarding eligibility for special education will be made at Violet’s upcoming IEP meeting.

Recommendations

* Explicit Comprehension Strategy Instruction: Provide direct instruction in identifying the question being asked, underlining key information, summarizing paragraphs in her own words, and generating brief written responses using sentence frames. Include guided practice before independent application.  
    
* Structured Word Problem Framework: Teach and consistently use a step-by-step problem-solving routine (e.g., read, restate, identify known/unknown, plan, solve, check). Provide visual templates or graphic organizers to reduce language load and support reasoning in multi-step math problems.  
    
* Modeling and Think-Alouds: Incorporate teacher modeling of reasoning processes in reading and math, making problem-solving steps visible. Gradually release responsibility with scaffolded practice before independent work.  
    
* Language Processing Supports: Provide simplified directions, chunk multi-step tasks into manageable parts, and check for understanding before work begins. Offer opportunities for brief one-on-one clarification before independent tasks.  
    
* Task Initiation and Organization Supports: Use assignment planners, checklists, or digital tracking tools with structured check-ins to support task initiation, follow-through, and completion of multi-step assignments.
