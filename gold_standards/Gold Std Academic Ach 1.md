*Reading:*  
In reading, Violet demonstrates solid word-level skills. Her Word Reading score fell in the Average range, and she used appropriate decoding strategies for unfamiliar words. She occasionally commented that items were difficult but was able to work through them accurately. Her Oral Reading Fluency score fell in the High Average range, indicating strong rate and accuracy when reading connected text aloud. However, her Reading Comprehension score fell in the Below Average range. During testing, she frequently relied on a look-back strategy to locate answers directly in the passage and often read verbatim rather than explaining ideas in her own words. Literal questions were answered more accurately than inferential or narrative questions. As passages increased in length and complexity, she became overwhelmed and unsure of what was being asked, particularly when questions were long or linguistically dense. This pattern suggests that while she can decode and read fluently, extracting meaning, synthesizing information, and responding to complex questions in her own words is significantly more difficult. In classroom settings, this may appear as strong oral reading but limited depth of understanding, especially when interpretation, inference, or written explanation is required.

*Math*:  
In mathematics, Violet’s Numerical Operations score fell in the Low Average range, while her Math Problem Solving score fell in the Below Average range. She demonstrated competence with basic computation, including multi-digit addition and subtraction, multiplication facts, multi-digit multiplication, simple algebra, adding fractions, and operations with negative integers. Finger counting was occasionally observed, though many basic facts appeared automatic. Division and multi-step equations were more difficult, and she was unable to complete long division or simplify more complex fraction problems. Her most significant difficulty emerged in word problems. She struggled with problems involving money, elapsed time, fractions in context, algebraic reasoning, perimeter, angles, and geometric interpretation. This pattern indicates that when math tasks are procedural and clearly structured, she can perform adequately. When language demands increase or when she must interpret the problem, identify relevant information, and determine a strategy independently, her performance declines. In the classroom, this is likely to affect performance on multi-step word problems, applied math tasks, and higher-level reasoning assignments more than basic computation drills.

*Written Language:*  
In written expression, Violet’s overall performance fell in the Average range, though with variability across tasks. On Sentence Composition, she demonstrated adequate ability to generate grammatically correct sentences with appropriate capitalization and punctuation. Sentence Building was stronger than Sentence Combining, where she sometimes omitted essential details when required to integrate multiple ideas into one concise sentence. Her Essay Composition score fell in the Average range and was a relative strength. When provided with a clear prompt and specific expectations, she wrote at length and stayed focused on the task. Her writing sample about Roblox demonstrated clear organization, multiple supporting reasons, and sustained development of ideas. Errors in capitalization, spelling, and word usage were present but did not obscure meaning. This pattern suggests that when expectations are explicit and the structure of the task is defined, she can produce extended written work successfully. However, tasks requiring synthesis of multiple ideas into concise responses or flexible organization may be more challenging.

Overall, Violet’s achievement profile shows solid foundational skills in word reading, basic computation, and structured writing, alongside clear difficulty with comprehension and applied reasoning. She performs well when tasks are explicit and clearly organized. When language is dense, problems are multi-step, or she must determine how to approach something independently, she becomes overwhelmed and uncertain. Her academic performance declines due to limitations in reasoning, interpretation, and organization of her thinking.
