Communication  
Quenton’s Communication standard score was 72 (3rd percentile).  His Receptive skills are at an age equivalent of approximately 11 years, 9 months. His Expressive skills are at approximately 4 years, 10 months, and Writing skills are at approximately 8 years, 1 month.  

Ratings show that Quenton understands more than he is able to say or write. Receptively, items endorsed indicate that he understands familiar vocabulary, follows routine directions, and responds appropriately to everyday verbal instructions. More complex receptive items, such as following multi-step directions without repetition or interpreting more abstract language, were rated as inconsistent or not demonstrated. Expressively, he is able to use spoken words to communicate basic wants and needs and respond to simple questions. However, items reflecting more advanced expressive language were not endorsed. These include clearly explaining ideas, describing events with detail, maintaining back-and-forth conversation, and answering complex or abstract questions.

Written communication ratings indicate emerging functional skills, including recognizing familiar words and demonstrating basic reading and writing behaviors. More advanced written items were rated as not demonstrated, including independently writing meaningful sentences, organizing written ideas logically, and reading more complex or grade-level text.

Daily Living Skills  
Quenton’s Daily Living Skills standard score was 78 (7th percentile). His Personal skills are at an age equivalent of 19  years or above. His Numeric skills are approximately 7 years 4 months. His School/Community skills are at approximately 8 years, 10 months.

Item-level ratings show that Quenton independently performs personal care tasks. Endorsed items include managing hygiene routines, dressing appropriately, caring for personal belongings, and completing familiar self-care responsibilities without assistance. These items were consistently rated as performed.

In the Numeric subdomain, he demonstrates basic number knowledge. Endorsed items include recognizing numbers, performing simple counting tasks, and completing basic number-related activities in structured situations. Items reflecting more advanced skills were rated as not demonstrated, including handling money independently in real-world situations, accurately calculating change, understanding time concepts such as elapsed time, and applying number skills flexibly outside of structured instruction.

In the School/Community subdomain, endorsed items indicate that he follows familiar school routines, transitions within known environments, and participates appropriately in structured settings. Items requiring independent community functioning, flexible problem solving, independent safety judgment, and managing broader responsibilities without adult guidance were not consistently endorsed.

Socialization  
Quenton’s Socialization standard score was 62 (1st percentile). His Interpersonal Relationships skills are at an age equivalent of approximately 3 years, 6 months. His Play and Leisure skills are approximately 5 years, 2 months. His Coping Skills are approximately 6 years, 6 months.

Item-level ratings indicate that Quenton shows interest in peers and participates in structured social activities. Endorsed items include engaging in familiar group routines, responding to adult direction during interactions, and participating in organized activities when expectations are clear.

Items reflecting more advanced social development were not endorsed. These include independently initiating and sustaining reciprocal conversations, interpreting subtle social cues, negotiating disagreements without adult support, adjusting behavior based on social context, and independently managing frustration across settings. Skills involving flexible social thinking, perspective taking, and independent conflict resolution were limited.
