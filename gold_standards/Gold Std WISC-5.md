This pattern is consistent with his difficulty coordinating memory and motor demands under time pressure. A significant weakness emerged in Blair’s overall working memory, where Blair scored in the Well Below Average range. Blair’s working memory weakness was consistent across digit span, sequencing, and other tasks that required him to hold and manipulate information. Blair’s pattern of reasoning strengths and processing weaknesses suggests that he may respond better when instruction is provided in small, explicit steps that reduce memory load and clarify the order in which information needs to be processed. His test behavior indicates that he may also need support with initiation, as he often hesitated or paused when unsure how to begin. He may participate more effectively when distractions are minimized and when adults are available to check for understanding before he moves forward.
